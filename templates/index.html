Methodology & Documentation
Raw Telemetry & Structural Baseline
Layer 1 · Orthographic Fingerprint

Every text leaves a physical signature in its character distribution, sentence rhythm, and word-length variance — patterns the conscious author rarely controls.

AI Probability Combines burstiness σ and lexical entropy into a 0–100% score. Values above 65% indicate statistically flat text consistent with LLM output. Burstiness σ Standard deviation of sentence lengths. Natural human writing is bursty (high σ). A σ below 2.0 is a strong AI-generation flag. Lexical Entropy Shannon entropy of the word distribution. Low entropy means repeated, predictable vocabulary — a sign of constrained or templated authorship.

The Driver Matrix (section C) shows which specific words were the heaviest contributors to each psychological cluster, ranked by weight and Z-score divergence.

Dissonance Radars
Layer 2 · Macro / Micro Conflict Detection

The engine separates cognition into two parallel channels: the Micro layer (subconscious letter-physics) and the Macro layer (conscious semantic framing). When these channels diverge beyond the threshold, a dissonance event is logged.

Micro · Subconscious BPV (Bio-Psycho-Vibrational) scores derived from letter shapes, pen pressure, and kinetic energy. Reflects the writer's autonomic state rather than deliberate intent. Macro · Conscious Semantic cluster scores across 6 axes — Power, Resources, Visibility, Temporal, Cognitive, Kinetic. Reflects deliberate word choice and conscious framing. Dissonance Alert Fires when |Z_macro − Z_micro| exceeds the Δ threshold. Three classifications: Psychological Fracture (>4σ), Posturing (macro > micro), or Suppressed Signal (micro > macro).
The Waveform Physics
Layer 2.5 · Oscilloscopic Signal Display

The text is treated as a physical signal. Each character's BPV value contributes energy to a running waveform — the same way audio amplitude is visualised on an oscilloscope.

Global Waveform 100-bucket bezier-smoothed envelope of the entire document's BPV energy. Flat lines suggest uniform authorship; sharp peaks indicate emotional escalation. Micro Oscilloscope 256-point raw BPV trace of the current analysis window — no smoothing. Allows detection of rhythmic patterns, repetition artefacts, or templated structure. Structural Morse Punctuation events plotted as a binary waveform. Dense punctuation clusters correlate with high emotional arousal or deliberate syntactic control. Wavelength Telemetry Full-document energy split across dominant frequency bands. Imbalance between low / mid / high BPV bands reveals tonal suppression or register manipulation.
Layer 3 · Somatic Cipher & FFT
Archetypal Letter Profiles & Steganographic Frequency Analysis

The Somatic Cipher maps the letter distribution to a 5-axis archetypal profile: Sovereign (authority), Origin (grounding), Kinetic (movement), Resonant (connection), Liminal (transition). These archetypes derive from the somatic geometries of the letterforms themselves.

Tier Badge T1 (Universal) to T5 (Extreme Cipher) — how far the letter distribution deviates from natural language norms. T4+ may indicate intentional encoding. Quersumme Digit-sum of the dominant BPV scores — a single-digit compression of the text's psychoacoustic fingerprint, used in comparative analysis. FFT Harmonics Fast Fourier Transform of the BPV waveform. Dominant frequency peaks can reveal periodic encoding, templated sentence injection, or machine-inserted steganographic payloads.

A flat or single-peaked FFT spectrum is normal for human writing. Multiple sharp harmonics above the noise floor suggest structured non-human authorship or covert data embedding.
